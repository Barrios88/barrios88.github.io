Media

Interviews, research features, and commentary covering work on entrepreneurship, labor markets, disclosure, and the economics of the firm.

Recent features

  • Yale Insights Feb 2026

    Remote Work Is Linked to a Decline in Financial Misconduct

    Coverage of research showing that firms better positioned for remote work saw sharp declines in financial misconduct during the pandemic—likely because remote arrangements raised the cost of sustaining collusion.

    Research feature
  • Yale Insights Oct 2025

    The Price of Trust: How Conflicts of Interest Threaten the Marketplace of Ideas

    Feature on experimental evidence that disclosed conflicts of interest erode trust in the institutions meant to produce independent knowledge.

    Research feature
  • Yale Insights Aug 2025

    Flexible Work Arrangements Made Workers Less Likely to Start Their Own Businesses

    Discussion of how employer-provided flexibility after COVID-19 changed who starts businesses—and dampened one traditional draw of entrepreneurship.

    Research feature
  • First Alert 4 Feb 2024

    Television interview on how delivery-platform economics have tightened for gig workers relative to the heavily subsidized early years of the industry.
