About

I am an Associate Professor of Accounting at the Yale School of Management and a Faculty Research Fellow at the National Bureau of Economic Research in the Political Economy and the Productivity, Innovation, and Entrepreneurship groups.

My research interests focus on the intersection of labor economics, entrepreneurship, and financial and managerial accounting. Specifically, my research has examined the areas of new business formation, culture and economic behaviors, human capital, financial reporting, regulation, managerial incentives, and corporate governance. My work has been published in leading accounting, economics, and finance journals such as the Journal of Accounting Research, the Journal of Public Economics, American Economic Journal: Microeconomics, Management Science, and the Journal of Financial Economics. My research has also been covered by The Economist, the Wall Street Journal, Fortune, among others — see Media.

I frequently present my research at major international conferences such as the American Economic Association Annual Meeting and the NBER Summer Institute. I currently serve as Editor of Accounting Horizons, Ad Hoc Advising Editor at the Journal of Accounting and Economics, and Guest Editor for the Journal of Labor Economics Symposium on Occupational Licensing. I am also the founder of the Labor and Accounting Group.

Before joining Yale SOM, I served as an Assistant Professor of Accounting and Fujimori/Mou Faculty Scholar at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and as an Assistant Professor at the Olin Business School at Washington University in St. Louis.

At Yale SOM, I teach Financial Accounting and Valuation and Private Firm CFO: Accounting and Finance Strategies. Outside of academia, I enjoy reading history, politics, cooking, and wine tasting.
